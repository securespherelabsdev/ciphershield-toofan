How It Works

1

You submit a tip

No name, no phone number, no account required. Just describe what you observed and where.

2

Your identity is deleted

Your IP address, device information, and the exact time of submission are permanently discarded at the moment your report arrives — before anything is stored.

3

Police receive a structured lead

Officers receive confidence-scored intelligence — zero reporter data attached. You can check your report's status using only a one-time code we give you.

What We Never Collect

This is not a policy. It is an architectural guarantee.

  • Your IP address
  • Your device or browser information
  • Your name, phone number, or contact details
  • Your precise location
  • Cookies or tracking identifiers
  • The time you submitted (we round to a 6-hour window)

"Even if compelled by a court order, we have nothing to hand over. This is not a policy. It is an architectural guarantee — the data does not exist at the point of receipt."

Who is this for?

If you are a citizen

Reporting is anonymous by design. There is no account to trace, no phone number to subpoena, no metadata to hand over. You can check your report status using only a one-time code — we do not know who you are.

If you are a law enforcement officer

CipherShield delivers structured, confidence-scored intelligence leads — not raw unverified tips. Each lead includes a credibility score, report type, district, and supporting details. Contact ciphershield@securespherelabs.com to request agency portal access.

What Happens to Your Report

You are not shouting into a void. Here is exactly what happens after you submit.

Received

Your report arrives. Your IP, device info, and timestamp are permanently deleted before anything is stored.

Scored

An automated credibility score (0–100) is assigned based on detail, district data, and corroboration. No human sees it yet.

Under Review

An intelligence officer reviews the lead. High-scoring reports are prioritised. The officer may request corroboration before escalating.

Dispatched

The lead is dispatched to field officers with an AI-generated intelligence briefing. Your report status updates to reflect this.

Investigated

Field officers act on the intelligence. You can check your report's status at any time using your anonymous token — no account needed.

Status updates may take 24–72 hours to reflect. Use your token at ciphershield.securespherelabs.com/status/ to track progress.

Questions People Are Afraid to Ask

Honest answers. No reassuring waffle.

Can police trace me through CipherShield?

No. The moment your report arrives, our server permanently deletes your IP address, device fingerprint, and exact submission time — before anything is written to disk. This is not a policy promise. It is an architectural guarantee baked into the code. Even if Kerala Police, a court, or any authority demanded your identity, we have nothing to hand over. The data never existed in our system.

You can verify this yourself: read the source code →

What if the government demands my identity?

We would comply with any lawful order — but we would have nothing to give. We cannot produce data we do not have. Your IP address is gone. Your device information is gone. The precise time you submitted is gone. A court order served to us would return empty hands.

This is by design. We believe anonymity should be technically enforced, not just legally promised.

Is visiting this site itself risky?

Over a normal connection, your Internet Service Provider can see that you visited ciphershield.securespherelabs.com — but not what you did or submitted here. HTTPS encrypts the content, not the destination.

If you want to hide even the site visit, use Tor Browser. CipherShield works fully over Tor with no modifications needed. See our Threat Model for a full breakdown of risks.

What happens after I submit?

Your report is assigned a credibility score and enters a review queue. An officer reviews it, and if it scores high enough, it is dispatched to field officers with an AI-generated intelligence briefing. You receive a one-time anonymous token you can use to check your report's status at any time — no account, no phone number, no identity required.

Is this legal to use?

Yes. Submitting an anonymous tip about suspected criminal activity is legal and encouraged. CipherShield is a civic reporting tool, not a channel for making false accusations — reports are credibility-scored precisely to filter out malicious or baseless tips.

If you are concerned about a specific legal question, consult a legal professional. We are not lawyers and this is not legal advice.

Who can see my report?

Only authorised officers who have been granted access to the CipherShield agency portal. Reports are encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM. Officers see the content of your report — what you observed, where, and when — but they see zero information about who submitted it, because that information was deleted before the report was ever stored.

Is CipherShield an official government product?

No. CipherShield is an independent civic technology initiative built by SecureSphereLabs. We have reached out to Kerala Police and relevant authorities and welcome formal collaboration, but no official agreement is currently in place. We make no claim of government endorsement. The platform is open source — anyone can audit it.

Built in the Open

CipherShield is fully open source. Every line of code that handles your report — from the moment it arrives to the moment it reaches law enforcement — is publicly auditable by security researchers, civil society organisations, and the press.

Security researchers: If you find a vulnerability in CipherShield, please disclose it responsibly to security@securespherelabs.com. We will acknowledge your report within 48 hours, work to fix it promptly, and credit you publicly if you wish. Please do not publish details before we have had a chance to patch.
