Can CipherShield Really Protect Me?

Honest answers about what we protect, what we cannot protect, and how to maximise your safety before you submit a report.

Protected

Your identity at submission

Your IP, device info, and submission time are deleted before anything is stored. We are architecturally incapable of handing them over.

Protected

Your report content at rest

All text you enter is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it is written to disk. The encryption key is never stored alongside the data.

Not Protected

What you write about yourself

If you include your name, phone number, or identifying details in the report text, those details are stored (encrypted, but present). We cannot protect you from yourself.

Partial

Network-level identity

By default, your ISP can see you visited this site. Use Tor Browser or a trusted VPN to prevent this. CipherShield is fully Tor-compatible.

Threat-by-Threat Breakdown

Threat Protected? Why
Server is seized / hacked Protected Your IP and device data were already deleted. Report text is encrypted at rest. There is no identity to recover.
Court order demanding reporter identity Protected We do not possess your identity. We cannot hand over data we do not have.
Database leak Protected All sensitive fields are AES-256-GCM encrypted. A leaked database reveals only ciphertext without the encryption key.
Your ISP watching your traffic Partial Your ISP can see you visited ciphershield.securespherelabs.com but not what you submitted (HTTPS). Use Tor Browser to hide even the site visit.
Someone watching your screen or device Not Protected CipherShield cannot protect you from physical observation or device-level surveillance. Clear your browser history after use.
You identify yourself in the report text Not Protected The content of your report is stored. If you include your name, neighbourhood, or other identifiers, those details exist in the system.
A compromised device (spyware / keylogger) Not Protected If malware is running on your device it can capture what you type before it ever reaches us. Use a clean, trusted device.
Someone you told about your report Not Protected Do not tell anyone you submitted a report. Social exposure is outside our threat model.

How Tor Improves Your Anonymity

CipherShield already deletes your IP address before storing anything. But your Internet Service Provider (ISP) can still see that you visited this website — even over HTTPS, the domain name is visible.

Using Tor Browser hides the site visit itself

When you use Tor Browser, your traffic is routed through three separate relays before reaching CipherShield. Your ISP only sees that you are using Tor — not which website you visited or what you submitted. This is the strongest network-level anonymity available.

CipherShield works over Tor without any modification. No JavaScript tricks, no WebRTC leaks, no external resources that could de-anonymise you. The entire page loads from a single origin with a strict Content Security Policy.

What We Cannot Protect You From

Do not do any of the following

  • Include your name, phone number, workplace, or any other personal detail in the report
  • Submit from a device you do not control (work device, shared family computer, internet café)
  • Submit on a network that monitors traffic (school Wi-Fi, office network)
  • Tell anyone — family, friends, colleagues — that you submitted a report
  • Take a screenshot of your submission token and store it somewhere linked to your identity
  • Access the status page from a different device where you are logged in to accounts

How to Submit as Safely as Possible

  • 1
    Use Tor Browser or a trusted VPN. This prevents your ISP from seeing you visited this site at all.
  • 2
    Use your personal device on a personal mobile data connection, not a shared or monitored network.
  • 3
    Do not mention yourself in the report. Describe what you saw — not who you are or how you know.
  • 4
    Write down your status token on paper and store it safely. Do not save it in a notes app, screenshot, or message thread tied to your identity.
  • 5
    Clear your browser history after submitting, or use a private / incognito window so the visit does not appear in your history.
  • 6
    Do not tell anyone you submitted a report. The greatest anonymity risk is social, not technical.

Why We Can Make These Claims

These are architectural guarantees, not policy promises. The anonymize middleware in our open-source backend runs before any route handler. It overwrites req.ip, all forwarded-for headers, the user-agent string, and the precise timestamp with empty values or a 6-hour-window approximation — before the report is ever processed or stored. Even if a developer wanted to log your IP, the data is already gone.
