Can CipherShield Really Protect Me?

Honest answers about what we protect, what we cannot protect, and how to maximise your safety before you submit a report.

Quick Verdict

Protected

Your identity at submission

Your IP address, device info, and exact time are deleted before anything is stored. We cannot hand them over — they don't exist.

Protected

Your report content at rest

All text you enter is encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it is written to disk. A leaked database reveals only ciphertext.

Partial

Network-level identity

Your ISP can see you visited this site over HTTPS. The content is hidden, but the domain name is not. Use Tor Browser to hide even the visit.

Not Protected

What you write about yourself

If you include your name, phone number, or other identifiers in the report text, those details are stored. We cannot protect you from yourself.

How Tor Browser Helps

CipherShield already deletes your IP address before storing anything. But your Internet Service Provider can still see the domain name you visited — even over HTTPS, the destination is visible at the network level.

Tor hides the site visit itself

When you use Tor Browser, your traffic passes through three separate encrypted relays before reaching CipherShield. Your ISP only sees that you are using Tor — not which site you visited or what you submitted. This is the strongest network-level anonymity available to a regular user.

CipherShield is fully Tor-compatible. No external scripts, no WebRTC, no third-party resources that could de-anonymise you. The entire page loads from a single origin with a strict Content Security Policy.

What We Cannot Protect You From

Avoid doing any of the following

  • Including your name, phone number, workplace, or any personal detail in the report
  • Submitting from a device you don't fully control — work laptop, shared family computer, café kiosk
  • Submitting on a monitored network — school Wi-Fi, employer network, hotel Wi-Fi
  • Telling anyone — family, friends, colleagues — that you submitted a report
  • Screenshotting your token and storing it somewhere tied to your identity
  • Checking your status from a device where you are logged into personal accounts

How to Submit as Safely as Possible

  • 1
    Use Tor Browser or a trusted VPN so your ISP cannot see which site you are visiting.
  • 2
    Submit from your personal device on mobile data — not a shared or monitored network.
  • 3
    Do not mention yourself in the report text. Describe what you saw, not who you are or how you know it.
  • 4
    Write your status token on paper and store it safely. Do not save it in a notes app, message thread, or screenshot linked to your identity.
  • 5
    Use a private / incognito window so the visit does not appear in your browser history, then close it when done.
  • 6
    Do not tell anyone you submitted a report. Social exposure is the most common real-world risk.

Why We Can Make These Claims

These are architectural guarantees backed by open source code — not policy promises. Our anonymize middleware runs before any route handler. It overwrites req.ip, all forwarded-for headers, the user-agent string, and the precise timestamp before the report is processed or stored. Even if a developer wanted to log your IP, the data is already gone by the time it reaches any route.
